## Synopsis
In Blackthorn County, the quiet streets are a stage for paranoia and hidden truths. Sienna Hale, an overextended lawyer, inherits a dusty portable classroom full of disciplinary secrets while trying to bury her own traumatic past. Her arrival stirs Tess Dane’s patient sommelier façade, who keeps keys to a church basement that might conceal more than just old hymn sheets. The town’s beloved vineyard, Blackthorn Vineyard, is the epicenter of whispered rumors about bees in winter and an unopened letter that could topple reputations. Meanwhile, Derek Sutton, the chaotic bartender friend, knows family secrets that threaten to unravel the town’s fabric—especially when a half-sibling rumor surfaces. Jenna Dane, the enigmatic therapist, watches from the church basement, her own knowledge of the orchard chapel’s closure adding layers of dread. As the story escalates through wine‑filled soirées and frantic drives to The Candle Finch, each character wrestles with trust that is not given but rented—leaving everyone perched on a precarious emotional cliff. By the finale, the ceramic owl resurfaces, the basement's ownership shuffles between condo, lake house, and abandoned vineyard, and a phone vibrates from inside a wall, promising yet another twist.

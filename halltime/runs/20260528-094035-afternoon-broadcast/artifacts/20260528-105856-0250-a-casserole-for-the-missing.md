## Synopsis

She suspects Mara Hale, an artisan developer whose candle‑lit retreat cabin exudes a healthy yet uncanny calm. Meanwhile, Tess Mercer, bartender extraordinaire, knows every whispered secret and carries an unresolved half‑sibling truth that could unravel the town’s fragile façade. The lake bell tolls without wind, hinting at unseen currents. A casserole dish becomes a symbolic artifact of community warmth turned deadly, while an unopened letter threatens to ignite old wounds. As secrets surface—an unsent email, a second phone, a mysterious brunch‑appearance—Wyatt navigates a web of suspicion, false accusations, and silent terror. The town’s façade cracks: the basement morphs from condo to lake house to abandoned vineyard, echoing the shifting reality that no one can anchor themselves in. Amidst wine‑drunk confessions and whispered warnings, the county’s past resurfaces, and a chilling question lingers—can love survive when arson lingers in the shadows?

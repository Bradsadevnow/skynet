## Synopsis

The space, filled with seasonal materials, becomes a labyrinth where every item whispers about secrets too heavy for daylight. When she moves into Bellweather—a locale that morphs between Vermont and Atlanta depending on how many glasses of wine she sips—she stumbles upon a snow globe that distorts time and a ceramic owl perched on a kitchen island, both artifacts that seem to observe her every move. The town’s residents are bound by an unspoken rule: trust is not given; it is rented, often at the price of a casserole or a whispered warning. Luke Bell, the marina manager with a suspiciously healthy handyman aura, becomes a reluctant ally and possible threat as he hides in a dock office that no one fully claims. Mara Bennett, the therapist with an elegant demeanor, holds the key to half‑siblings and brunches with deceased patrons. The narrative weaves through scenes of wine, mud on boat keys, and basement lights flickering like distant memories. Each act escalates the tension as secrets are unearthed in kitchens, cabins, and side rooms filled with old family records, culminating in a climax that blurs reality and folklore. The story leaves viewers questioning whether the town’s collective silence is a shield or a cage, while hinting at a deeper conspiracy tied to the Blackthorn Vineyard.

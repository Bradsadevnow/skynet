## Synopsis
Cole Dane arrives in Alder Ridge after a quiet, emotionally negligent implosion that left her a magazine editor and a dock office she never claimed ownership of. The town’s streets hum with whispers of a vanished vineyard fire and the blood‑adjacent tea towel that once draped a table at the Marigold Inn. Every corner seems to conceal a basement—some legal, some emotional. Cole's search for truth leads her into meditation annexes nobody uses in winter, into secret emails that could shatter families, and into a boat key that glistens with fresh mud. The town’s residents are layered: grief counselors whispering alibis, real estate agents hiding casserole evidence, therapists holding inherited documents, physicians who know where evidence should have been buried, and teachers haunted by wrongful accusations. Through each scene, the narrative unspools a web of suspicion rather than proof, turning every basement into a character. The climax erupts at Bellweather’s office with ambulance lights, an empowerment speech, and a locked door that may hold the key to the town’s collective fear. Yet even as secrets surface, contradictions grow—names drift, geography blurs, mortality blames a dead friend, and the basement shifts ownership from condo to lake house to abandoned vineyard—leaving the audience questioning what is real and what is just fear wearing cashmere.

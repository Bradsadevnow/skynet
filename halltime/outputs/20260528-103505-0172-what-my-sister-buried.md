## Synopsis
In Blackthorn County’s fog‑drenched streets, Mara Pike steps into Marigold Falls, a town that cradles secrets like old churches cradle pews. She inherits the church basement—an overflowing donation cellar—while her career as a lawyer burns brighter than any confession she could ever deliver. Her sister’s funeral was never truly over; it was buried in layers of unopened letters and hidden casserole dishes, each one whispering that family can be both sanctuary and crime scene. The county’s inhabitants are bound by an unseen static on their phones, calling her name like a warning from the past. A storm of distrust erupts when Gavin Hale, a charismatic hospital administrator with a dock office that nobody truly claims, enters the picture. Mara finds herself tangled in a web where career ambition clashes with inherited guilt, and every clue—scarves with impossible timing, unsealed letters, and the scent of burnt wine—pulls her deeper into a gothic divorce plot that could never be neatly wrapped up.

## Synopsis
Tess Marlowe, a lawyer whose career has eclipsed every personal corner she touches, arrives at Alder Ridge after an emotionally negligent implosion that left her both adrift and determined to keep secrets hidden in plain sight. The town’s marina tackle room—merely storage on paper—is where she inherits a boat key, a ceramic owl, and a wet Christmas ornament, each item echoing the undercurrent of trust rented rather than given. Evelyn Mercer, an attorney with no visible clients, hides a storage unit he refuses to explain; Rachel Cross, bartender extraordinaire, knows more family secrets than her own children; Derek Hale, elegant widow of a foundation, keeps documents she should have burned; and Cole Cross, rugged vineyard groundskeeper, still carries the silence of a broken engagement. The narrative weaves through wine-soaked scenes, basement lights flickering like legal candles, and a town that never truly forgets—only redecorates its memories in containers labeled “just storage.” As accusations rise and truths unravel, Tess must confront her own buried trauma while the community’s collective guilt threatens to drown them all. In the final crescendo, the ceramic owl returns to the kitchen island, the basement’s door locks shut, and a phone vibrates from inside a wall—an ominous reminder that even in Blackthorn County, nothing is truly finished.

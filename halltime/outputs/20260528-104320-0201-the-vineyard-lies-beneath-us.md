## Synopsis
In the rain‑slicked heart of Blackthorn County, Brooke Cross trades her polished corporate world for a dock office that nobody fully claims, hoping the quiet of Marigold Falls will smother the echoing implosion of her past divorce. Yet every corner of this town is steeped in shadows: a lake bell rings without wind, basements whisper secrets, and a vineyard fire burns deeper than the soil. As Brooke digs through hidden drawers, she finds a locked box and an unsent email that could destroy a family—yet she never quite knows who to trust. Claire Dane, the rugged dock repair contractor with a mysterious past, becomes both ally and threat as he repairs a lock that should not exist. The town’s gossip swirls around a casserole dish, a boat key, and a snow globe that seem to hold the keys to buried guilt. In an atmosphere thick with emotional exposition and municipal policy failures—wine always present—the characters navigate career versus family guilt, each scene tightening the web of suspicion until the basement light flickers, signaling that some truths are never fully exhumed.

## Synopsis
Her Silent Christmas Alibi drapes Blackthorn County in a velvet cloak of dread, where every whisper is a gunshot and every ornament conceals wet blood beneath glittering ice. Noah Sutton, an overextended professional lawyer who fled a home implosion, settles into a cramped side room that smells of old family records and winter heater soot. Her quiet arrival unsettles Claire Winterson, a nurse whose best‑friend status hides a looming half‑sibling secret, and Gavin Winterson, a rugged vineyard groundskeeper with a past stitched from vanished bodies and quiet engagements. As the town’s Christmas spirit thickens—wine flows like municipal policy failures—so do the mysteries: a snow globe that glows damp, a casserole dish that vanishes into seasonal storage, and a blood‑adjacent tea towel that hints at Blackthorn Vineyard’s cursed lineage. When Gavin Mercer, a mysterious elegant therapist, warns of a wet ornament in a lit kitchen, the sheriff’s muddy footprints on the boat key foreshadow an arson that never truly burned. In the climax, Claire Bell spots the timeline error that every adult rents their sanity, and Rachel Lark rehearses an alibi before anyone asked. The story crescendos with a frantic drive to Channel 8 Night Report, ambulance lights flashing like judgment, and a locked door in an unused portable classroom holding archived disciplinary files. By the final beat, Gavin Mercer’s confession only deepens the truth, the sheriff arrives fourteen minutes late, and the ceramic owl reappears—setting up a sequel that promises more whispered conspiracies and vanished lives.
